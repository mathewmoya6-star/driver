📖 Course Structure

📘 PART 1: FOUNDATION (6 Hours)

▼

Unit 1.1: Introduction to Driving

  • Traffic safety in East Africa (3,000+ deaths annually, 83% human error)
  • Driver tasks: Observe → Decide → Act
  • Legal framework: Traffic Act Cap 403, NTSA Act
  • Vehicle categories A to G with age requirements

Unit 1.2: Traffic Laws & Regulations

  • Speed limits: 50 km/h built-up, 110 km/h highway
  • 8-hour driving limit (Traffic Act Section 66A)
  • Zero tolerance for PSV drivers (0.00% BAC)
  • Penalties for drunk driving, no insurance, overloading

Unit 1.3: Driver Responsibilities & Ethics

  • Road courtesy: patience, positive attitude, no road rage
  • Vulnerable road users: children, elderly, disabled, cyclists
  • Eco-driving: smooth acceleration, proper tyre pressure
  • Fatigue management: rest every 2-3 hours

🛣️ PART 2: ROAD USER KNOWLEDGE (6 Hours)

▼

Unit 2.1: Traffic Signs & Signals

  • Triangle = Warning | Circle = Order | Rectangle = Information
  • Regulatory signs: speed limits, no entry, no overtaking
  • Warning signs: bends, slippery road, children crossing
  • Traffic lights: RED (stop), RED+AMBER (stop, prepare), GREEN (go), AMBER (stop if safe)
  • Police hand signals and traffic marshal signals

Unit 2.2: Road Markings & Right of Way

  • Yellow continuous line = no overtaking
  • Yellow broken line = overtaking if clear
  • White continuous line = no lane changing
  • Yellow kerb = no parking, no stopping

Unit 2.3: Model Town Board

  • One-way roads: white lines, overtaking rules, central reserve
  • Roundabout: give way to right, no stopping, no lane change
  • Lane options on four-lane road (Lane 1-4)
  • Angle parking: forward in, reverse out | Flush parking: reverse in, forward out

🚗 PART 3: VEHICLE OPERATION (4 Hours)

▼

Unit 3.1: Vehicle Controls & Instruments

  • Primary controls: steering wheel, accelerator, brake, clutch, gear lever, handbrake
  • Dashboard instruments: speedometer, tachometer, temperature gauge, warning lights
  • ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) prevents wheel lock
  • SRS (Secondary Restraint System) = airbags

Unit 3.2: Basic Vehicle Handling

  • Starting procedure: check mirrors, neutral gear, clutch, start engine
  • Gear selection: 1st (0-30 km/h), 2nd (15-50), 3rd (30-70), 4th (50-110), 5th (80+)
  • Steering: 9 and 3 position for airbag vehicles, pull-push method
  • Pre-trip inspection: tyres, lights, fluids, brakes, emergency equipment

🛡️ PART 4: SAFE DRIVING (6 Hours)

▼

Unit 4.1: Speed & Space Management

  • 4-Second Rule: following distance in adverse weather
  • Stopping distance = Thinking distance + Braking distance
  • Freewheeling (coasting) reduces control, does NOT save fuel
  • MSM technique: Mirror → Signal → Manoeuvre

Unit 4.2: Adverse Conditions & Emergencies

  • Night driving: low beams within 150m, don't over-drive headlights
  • Fog: low beams, fog lights, pull off if visibility drops
  • Rain: slippery first 10 minutes, increase following distance
  • Skid recovery: feet off pedals, steer where you want to go
  • Brake failure: pump brake, apply handbrake gently

Unit 4.3: First Aid & Accident Procedures

  • Dr. A.B.C: Danger, Response, Airway, Breathing, Circulation
  • Recovery position for unconscious but breathing casualty
  • Bleeding control: firm pressure, raise limb
  • Burns: cool with running water for 20 minutes
  • Choking: Heimlich manoeuvre (inward and upward thrusts)
  • At crash scene: reflector triangles 50m ahead/behind, call 999, don't move injured unless fire
